# Channel-Neutral Argument Template

Use this structure when developing an approved brief angle into a channel-neutral argument.

## Central Claim

Restate the approved angle as a clear, defensible assertion — not a question or aspiration. This is the one main idea the argument will carry forward. (1-2 sentences)

## Reasoning

Explain the logic or causal chain that connects the evidence to the central claim. What makes this claim defensible? Why does the evidence support this specific view? (2-3 sentences)

## Evidence Mapping

For each major supporting point, cite the supplied data or examples that carry it:

- **Point 1**: (The reasoning or claim this point supports)
  - Supporting evidence: (Which supplied data, case detail, metric, or quote supports this)
  - Source or reference: (Where this evidence comes from in the brief or lead comments)

- **Point 2**: (The reasoning or claim this point supports)
  - Supporting evidence: (Which supplied data, case detail, metric, or quote supports this)
  - Source or reference: (Where this evidence comes from in the brief or lead comments)

- **Point 3** (if applicable): (The reasoning or claim this point supports)
  - Supporting evidence: (Which supplied data, case detail, metric, or quote supports this)
  - Source or reference: (Where this evidence comes from in the brief or lead comments)

## Trade-Offs and Implications

Name any tensions, constraints, or downstream effects visible in the evidence. What are the limits or qualifications of this claim? What trade-off or friction is present?

- **Trade-off 1**: (What is the tension or constraint?)
  - Why it matters: (How does this affect the argument?)

- **Implication 1**: (What follows if this claim is true?)
  - For the reader: (What should they take away or consider?)

## Direction

State plainly what the reader should take away or consider based on this argument. What action, belief, or understanding does the argument support? (1-2 sentences)

## Validation Checklist

Before closing, verify:

- [ ] Approved angle is clearly developed (not generic or shifted from Brief Review)
- [ ] Central claim is specific and defensible from supplied evidence
- [ ] Every evidence point is mapped to a supporting claim
- [ ] No new evidence, metrics, or causal claims are invented
- [ ] Trade-offs or tensions are named when present in the evidence
- [ ] Argument avoids LinkedIn formatting, hashtags, emojis, or platform-specific structure
- [ ] Argument respects Wolven voice: straightforward, friendly, imaginative, confident
- [ ] Every factual claim traces to supplied evidence
- [ ] Argument is substantial and clear enough for downstream channel adaptation
