# Editorial Brief Template

Use this structure when creating investigative briefs for content development.

## Communication Objective

What should readers understand, believe, discuss, or do after engaging with content on this topic? (1-2 sentences)

## Central Claim

What specific claim, point of view, or insight is Wolven defending? State it as a defensible, concrete statement, not a question or a vague aspiration. (1-2 sentences)

## Evidence Inventory

List the concrete, supplied materials available to carry this brief:

- **Data points and metrics**: (results, KPIs, benchmarks supplied in the input)
- **Case details or examples**: (specific client work, decisions, outcomes)
- **Decisions and reasoning**: (why something was chosen, what was learned)
- **Observations**: (patterns, learnings, perspectives from experience)
- **Quotes or named sources**: (publication references, verified statements)
- **Links or publications**: (URLs, external sources with exact titles)

## Identified Gaps

What is missing from supplied material that might strengthen the brief?

- **Causal proof**: Does the brief explain why this matters?
- **Comparisons**: Is there a benchmark or competing approach?
- **Counter-arguments**: What trade-offs or tensions exist?
- **Stakeholder perspective**: Is there a voice or perspective missing?
- **External validation**: Is this insight unique to Wolven or validated elsewhere?

- **Core claim**: (The main idea for this angle)
- **Evidence lens**: (Which supplied data carries this angle)
- **Hook**: (Opening framing or entry point)
- **Tension**: (Trade-off, decision, or friction point visible in the evidence)
- **Direction**: (What readers should take away)

## Recommended Focus

Based on evidence strength and relevance to Wolven's positioning, recommend one angle for development in the next stage.

- **Selected angle**: (Angle 1, 2, or 3)
- **Why**: (Strength of evidence, defensibility, and alignment with communication objective)
