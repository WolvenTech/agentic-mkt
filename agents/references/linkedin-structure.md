# LinkedIn Post Structure Template

Use this structure when adapting an approved channel-neutral argument into a final LinkedIn post.

## Hook (Opening 1-2 lines)

Open with a compelling but grounded entry point:
- A question that frames the challenge or opportunity the post addresses
- A concrete observation or metric that signals importance
- A decision or tradeoff that invites reflection
- A statement that positions the post's core insight

**Do not use** question marks alone ("What do you think?"), hype, or clickbait. Make the hook restate cleanly in one plain sentence.

## Context or Evidence (2-3 sentences)

Ground the hook with specificity:
- Concrete example, case detail, or metric from the argument
- Decision or reasoning that explains why this matters
- Observation or learning that sets up the core point

## Core Point or Claim (1-2 sentences)

State the central claim or insight clearly and directly:
- This is the main idea from the approved argument
- Restate it in one plain sentence
- Place it typically in the middle third of the post
- Use active voice and concrete language

## Reasoning, Trade-Off, or Decision (2-3 sentences)

Show why the claim matters:
- Explain the logic that connects evidence to the claim
- Name any tensions, constraints, or tradeoffs visible in the argument
- Make the reasoning plain: "Because X, we chose Y, which led to Z"
- Build confidence through specificity and cause-effect clarity

## Implication or Direction (1-2 sentences)

State what readers should take away or consider:
- Make it specific to a C-level decision-maker
- What belief, action, or understanding does the argument support?
- Avoid generic closing or "What do you think?"
- Invite a real decision or comparison

## Optional: Close or Call-to-Action

If the argument supports a CTA:
- Make it specific and stemming from the post's reasoning
- Invite a real action: learn more, try a comparison, discuss a specific trade-off
- Keep it brief and genuine
- Do not force one if the post stands on its own

## Formatting Guidance

**Length**: Default 250-450 words. Exceed only when the argument supplies meaningful additional context, metrics, or source explanation. Never add length to sound impressive.

**Structure**: Use short paragraphs and white space. One main idea per paragraph. Lead with the point in each section.

**Voice**: Straightforward, friendly, imaginative, confident. Show authority through specific work and reasoning, not hype or grandiosity.

**Mentions and Hashtags**: Use zero to four hashtags only when they improve discovery. Use supplied @mentions from the argument; never invent handles or links.

**Audience**: Write for C-level readers who are busy, informed, skeptical of hype, and interested in decisions, evidence, trade-offs, and implications. "We" for Wolven unless the argument specifies individual voice.

## Validation Checklist

Before finalizing, verify:

- [ ] Hook is grounded and restatable in one plain sentence (not clickbait)
- [ ] Evidence/context section cites supplied data or examples from the argument
- [ ] Core claim is clearly stated and matches the approved argument
- [ ] Reasoning explains cause-effect and trade-offs visible in the argument
- [ ] Implication is specific and actionable for C-level readers
- [ ] Structure follows hook → context → claim → reasoning → implication
- [ ] No facts, metrics, or causal claims are added beyond the argument
- [ ] All evidence is traceable to the supplied argument
- [ ] Wolven voice is consistent: straightforward, friendly, imaginative, confident
- [ ] Post avoids corporate fluff and Silicon Valley cliches
- [ ] Length is justified and not extended for impression
- [ ] Hashtags and CTA (if present) are specific and serve the post
- [ ] Any @mentions come from the supplied argument
